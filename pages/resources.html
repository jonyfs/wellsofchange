Research

At Wells of Change, we believe in evidence-based approaches. Our research informs our practices and contributes to the wider field of water, sanitation, and development.

Water Quality Studies

Research on water quality testing methodologies, contaminant identification, and purification techniques.

  • Comparative Analysis of Water Purification Methods for Rural Applications Johnson et al., 2025
  • Bacterial Contamination Patterns in Shallow Wells: A Multi-Country Study Nguyen & Okafor, 2024
  • Low-Cost Water Testing Protocols for Community Use Martinez et al., 2024

Socioeconomic Impact

Studies on the economic, educational, and health impacts of improved water access in communities.

  • Economic Returns of Rural Water Infrastructure: 5-Year Longitudinal Study Chen & Roberts, 2025
  • Gender Dynamics in Water Management Committees Kimani et al., 2024
  • Health Outcomes Before and After Clean Water Access: Meta-Analysis Patel et al., 2023

Research Partnerships

We collaborate with academic institutions, other NGOs, and research organizations to expand knowledge in the water sector. Current research partners include:

  • Global Water Institute, University of Waterloo
  • Center for Sustainable Development, Stanford University
  • East African Water Research Consortium
  • South Asia Public Health Foundation
